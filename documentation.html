About the Project

This documentation provides the methodological framework underlying PHIDE (Philosophical Digital Edition Catalogue). PHIDE aims to offer a comprehensive overview of the current landscape of digital scholarly editions in philosophy, highlighting their features and contributions to the field.

The primary goal of PHIDE is to give scholars and researchers a structured entry point to discover digital editions, facilitating their integration into various stages of the scholarly workflow.

PHIDE was originally conceived as part of my thesis project. The current version of PHIDE benefits from a data model aligned with FAIR principles for digital editions.

Through this catalogue, researchers can explore the ecosystem of philosophical digital editions, compare editorial practices, and identify tools and methodologies relevant to their own scholarly projects.

Navigation Structure

The main navigation bar includes the Browse menu, which provides access to two sections: Schedule and Catalogue.

Schedule

The Schedule section provides a tabular overview of the digital scholarly editions included in PHIDE. It is structured as a dataset that can be browsed, filtered, and exported in CSV, JSON, and Excel formats.

Catalogue

The catalogue is organized as a searchable database accessible through filters and descriptive records. Each record represents a digital scholarly resource.

Main filters include:

  • Sort: ordering of results (by title, author, curator)
  • Language: language of the resource
  • Epoch: historical period
  • Advanced Search: combined search criteria such as index, glossary, facsimiles, and tools.

Each record may include direct links to external resources (the website of the digital edition), the Schedule entry, and the geographical localization of the project via the interactive map.

Selection Criteria

The selection process was initially guided by reliable and up-to-date sources, including Patrick Sahle’s Digital Editions Browser (last updated 2026), the Catalogue of Digital Editions by Greta Franzini (last updated 2022), and RIDE: A Review Journal for Scholarly Digital Editions and Resources.

PHIDE follows the approach proposed by Patrick Sahle for cataloguing digital scholarly editions, focusing on the editions themselves and excluding digital libraries, pedagogical resources, and projects that do not aim to produce scholarly editions.

Accessibility represents a fundamental criterion: all editions included in PHIDE must be available online, preferably with persistent links and structured metadata.
