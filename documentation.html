About the Project

This documentation provides the methodological framework underlying PHIDE (Philosophical Digital Edition Catalogue). PHIDE aims to offer a comprehensive and structured point of access to the current landscape of digital scholarly editions in philosophy, highlighting their distinctive features and their contribution to the field.

PHIDE was developed within the framework of my thesis project and originated from an empirical investigation conducted during its initial phase. The catalogue was designed on the basis of the results of a structured questionnaire administered to 72 philosophy students enrolled in Bachelor’s, Master’s, and PhD programmes at Italian universities.
The survey sought to assess students’ level of familiarity with digital scholarly editions (DSEs), their patterns of use, and their expectations regarding digital tools for research and academic study. The findings revealed a significant gap between the widespread use of digital academic resources and the still limited use of digital scholarly editions. Although students regularly rely on digital materials, DSEs remain comparatively underutilized. At the same time, the vast majority of respondents expressed interest in a dedicated platform that collects and describes available philosophical digital scholarly editions, thereby confirming the need for a structured point of access.

Through this catalogue, researchers can explore the ecosystem of philosophical digital editions, compare editorial practices, and identify tools and methodologies relevant to their own research projects. The current version of PHIDE is based on a data model aligned with the FAIR principles principles for digital editions.

Navigation Structure

The main navigation bar includes the Browse menu, which provides access to two sections: Table of Contents and Catalogue.

Table of Contents

The Table of Contents section provides a tabular overview of the digital scholarly editions included in PHIDE. It is structured as a dataset that can be browsed, filtered, and exported in CSV, JSON, and Excel formats.

Catalogue

The catalogue is organized as a searchable database accessible through filters and descriptive records. Each record represents a digital scholarly resource.

Main filters include:

  • Sort: ordering of results (by title, author, curator)
  • Language: language of the resource
  • Epoch: historical period
  • Advanced Search: combined search criteria such as index, glossary, facsimiles, and tools.

Each record may include direct links to external resources (the website of the digital edition), the Description entry, and the geographical localization of the project via the interactive map.

Map

The Map section provides an interactive visualization of the geographical distribution of all editions in PHIDE. Users can zoom and pan across the map, click on markers to view edition details and access direct links to the edition's website. This map is fully integrated with the catalogue: within each specific edition's record in the Catalogue, a link is available that highlights the corresponding location on the map. This allows users to switch seamlessly between the catalogue entry and its geographical context.

Selection Criteria

The selection process was initially guided by reliable and up-to-date sources, including Patrick Sahle's Digital Editions Browser (last updated 2026), the Catalogue of Digital Editions by Greta Franzini (last updated 2022), and RIDE: A Review Journal for Scholarly Digital Editions and Resources. Regarding the section on tools, the project was also informed by DSE Tools, a catalog of software tools for digital philology compiled by Chiara Martignano, which provided guidance on identifying relevant technical environments and software used in digital scholarly editions.

PHIDE follows the approach proposed by Patrick Sahle for cataloguing digital scholarly editions, focusing on the editions themselves and excluding digital libraries, pedagogical resources, and projects that do not aim to produce scholarly editions.

Accessibility represents a fundamental criterion: all editions included in PHIDE must be available online, preferably with persistent links and structured metadata.
